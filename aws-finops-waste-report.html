Prioritize by ease of deletion against potential savings. "Safe" items are reversible or non-impacting; "Approval" items can break workloads and need a stakeholder sign-off.

Rule of thumb: "Safe to delete" = unattached storage, released IPs, snapshots with a retention copy, and policy-driven lifecycle changes. "Requires approval" = anything attached to a running workload, networking path, database, or a financial commitment.

6 · Remediation Plan — Detect → Notify → Terminate

A non-disruptive workflow that gives engineering teams a window to object before anything is deleted.

STEP 1

Detect

  • Run the criteria in §3 on a schedule (Tenable findings + AWS Config / Cost Explorer / CloudWatch).
  • Auto-tag candidates finops:review=YYYY-MM-DD.
  • Exclude DR, batch, and tag finops:keep=true resources.
STEP 2

Notify

  • Route each candidate to its owner tag via Slack/Jira/email.
  • Give a 7–14 day grace window to reclaim or justify.
  • Snapshot-then-stop reversible items; keep an audit trail.
STEP 3

Terminate

  • Auto-delete only "Safe" items after grace + snapshot.
  • "Approval" items require ticket sign-off before deletion.
  • Record realized savings; feed back into §5 assumptions.
